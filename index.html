  1. Dark Web Crawler Tor - Detection Of Drug Sales

    A Dark Web Crawler is a specialized web crawler designed to navigate the Dark Web, which is a part of the internet that is not indexed by traditional search engines and is only accessible through special software such as Tor. The Dark Web is known for being a haven for illegal activities such as drug trafficking, human trafficking, and cybercrime, making it an area of interest for law enforcement agencies and security researchers. Dark Web Crawlers work by using Tor or other anonymizing networks to access Dark Web sites and collect information on them. They are often used by security researchers to monitor illegal activities and identify potential threats to cybersecurity.


    1. Grapical Password Authentication

      Graphical Password Authentication (GPA) is a method of user authentication that uses images, rather than traditional alphanumeric passwords, to grant access to a system or device. This method of authentication is becoming increasingly popular due to its ease of use, enhanced security, and ability to create memorable passwords that are harder to guess or hack. GPA typically involves the user selecting a series of images from a grid or set of images to create a unique pattern or sequence, which serves as their password. The images may be randomly generated or selected from a predefined set of images, such as faces, objects, or landscapes.
